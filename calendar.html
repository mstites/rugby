Zines and Writing

A small sample of the zines I have written. These zines focus on my experiences with gender discrimination and dysphoria. I plan on creating new poetry zines as well as compiling many of my existing writings into zine format.

Please feel free to make copies (for non commercial purposes) and distribute as long as they include credit and are not altered. Please reach out with suggestions or if interested in collaborative work.

Dear Rugby

An open letter to USA Rugby and more widely the systematic transphobia that prevents me from engaging in sports like everyone else.

In this zine, I air my frustrations navigating sports as a trans woman and address many of the common arguments against allowing trans women their rightful place in women's sports.

Euphoria

Joins the Dysphoria and Transphobia zines as the final piece in the series.

I wanted "Euphoria" to provide a positive ending and bring a sense of conclusion to the series. In the end, I focused the zine on my exploration finding positivity, pride, and joy in being transgender despite all the hardship.

Transphobia

A collection of poetry and short essays formed in the fashion of letters addressed to specific forms of harassment and oppression I have experienced as a trans woman.

The second zine in a series of three zines, joined by Dysphoria and Euphoria. This zine in particular focused on addressing deep rooted trauma.

Dysphoria

A collection of some of the first gender poetry I ever wrote. I originally hesitated uploading this zine to the site, as I was still very early in my trans journey and still very confused on where I was headed.

However, I decided it is important not to be ashamed or try to hide any part of my trans experience. Confusion and change are often essential steps of gender exploration - and I want to help normalize that.

The Dysphoria zine ended up turning into a three zine series, later joined by Transphobia and Euphoria.
